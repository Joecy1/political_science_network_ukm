## Curation of content: search and retrieval 
One core question we need to ask about the website is: when we have a lot of AI tools available in the market, when we have Google research, we have a lot of good products out there. For example, Research Rabbit, Zotero, we have Google Scholar. How does our website differ from the other product on the digital? How can we add value to our user?

I think one part is about our curation of content and how we can help them to narrow down their search or to scope down their search in a certain way. If there are some papers that are seminal for our syllabus, people can find them on the website and people can read the reviews of one another. It's a good place where we can communicate and critique our points of view from there. 

## Communicative Action and Cooperation 
The second part about the website is upon the same issue: are we able to find a different perspective to view the same issue? Is there different interests involved for the same issue, and are there different stakeholders involved? Are we able to find a common ground for the same issue and work toward it together and cooperate? If we have to compete against one another, how can we engage in a competition where it is at least a zero-sum and not a negative-sum, where we are just racing to the bottom? At the end of it, no one gets benefits; it's kind of a prisoner's dilemma. How can we coordinate this kind of cooperation and competition, having one generation helping another generation so that they do not have to reinvent the wheel again.
