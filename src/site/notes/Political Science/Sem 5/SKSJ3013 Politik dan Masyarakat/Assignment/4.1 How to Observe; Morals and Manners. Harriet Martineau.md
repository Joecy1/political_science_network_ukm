Upon reflections on my methods to observe, I have come across Harriet Martineau's *How to Observe: Morals and Manners.* This book contains more guides on how to observe and what to observe. She had cautious the travelers to not generalise too quick.

"As long as travelers continue to neglect the safe means of generalization which are within the reach of all and build theories upon the manifestations of individual minds, there is little hope of inspiring men with that spirit of impartiality, mutual deference, and love, which are the best enlighteners of the eyes and rectifiers of the understanding."
- p.19

---
>[!Note] In her *Theory and Practice of Democracy in America*, she has observed a different types of democracy than Alexis de Tocqueville(1805-1859), even though they in the same era. 

"No books are so little to be trusted as travels. Most … take a fact for a principle…; and the quickest minds which love to reason and explain more than to observe with patience, go most astray... Still I think travels useful. Different accounts give means of approximation to truth; and by-and-by, what is fixed and essential in a people will be brought out."
