The website is deliberate organised in certain way that is unique among all the political science sources out there. 

One reason I created the website is to mimic the experience of reading a book. This guide drew inspiration from *How to Read A Book*, *Information Anxiety*, *The Organised Mind*, *Gaya UKM*, *How to Write a Thesis*, *Literature Search*, *How to Take Smart Notes*, *Building a Second Brain*, and *The Extended Mind*. 

Another reason why do I organised them is to encourage conversation. In another words, a syntopical reading or a seminar. According to *How to Read A Book*, there are four types of reading. 

It is organised by *Map of Content (MOC)*. It is similar to a book table of content. But a book's table of content is set in stone, once it is published. A map allows you to move freely within books, between books, and most importantly out of books. 

*Copyright page, Preface, Title, Section, Index, References* are often thing that people do not pay attention to. But if you do, they can offer you quicker way to find what you need and you can engage in the conversations in a more meaningful way.

It is organised by the convention of *Gaya UKM* to name books and articles.
