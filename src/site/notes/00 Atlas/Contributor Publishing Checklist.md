# Contributor Publishing Checklist

## 1. Contributor Verification
- Full name or consistent pseudonym provided
- Short bio (who you are, what you work on)
- At least 1 previous work sample (link or file)
- Conflict of interest disclosed
- Agreed to platform guidelines and publishing terms

---

## 2. Content Fit
- Topic is relevant to the website’s focus
- Clear category selected (e.g., politics, economics, society)
- Target audience defined
- Purpose is clear:
  - Inform
  - Analyze
  - Argue
  - Summarize research
- Content answers a clear question or problem

---

## 3. Structure Requirements
- Title is clear and specific (not vague or clickbait)
- Summary/abstract included (100–200 words)
- Clear thesis or main idea
- Logical flow:
  - Context
  - Argument
  - Evidence
  - Conclusion
- Proper headings used
- Optional: 3–5 key insights

---

## 4. Evidence and Rigor
- Claims are supported with evidence
- Sources are credible (journals, official data, primary sources)
- All sources are cited
- No unsupported claims
- Clear distinction between:
  - Fact
  - Interpretation
  - Opinion
- Optional: Counterarguments addressed
- Optional: Limitations acknowledged

---

## 5. Originality
- Content is original
- No plagiarism
- AI-assisted content is edited and verified
- Proper attribution for all references

---

## 6. Clarity and Writing Quality
- Language is clear and precise
- Minimal unnecessary jargon
- Grammar and spelling acceptable
- Paragraphs readable (not overly long)
- Tone matches platform style

---

## 7. Ethics and Compliance
- No defamatory or unverified accusations
- No harmful or illegal content
- Sensitive topics handled responsibly
- Sponsorships or incentives disclosed

---

## 8. SEO (Optional)
- Meta description provided
- Relevant keywords included
- Tags/categories selected
- Internal links added (if applicable)

---

## 9. Editorial Process
Status flow:
1. Submitted
2. Screening (fit + basic quality)
3. Editorial review
4. Revision (if needed)
5. Approved
6. Published

Each submission must:
- Pass minimum quality threshold
- Be reviewed before publishing

---

## 10. Post-Publication
- Article open for feedback/comments
- Author may respond to feedback
- Content reviewed periodically for relevance
- Track performance (views, engagement)

---

## 11. Contributor Tracking
Track per contributor:
- Number of submissions
- Acceptance rate
- Quality score (internal)

Use this to:
- Fast-track strong contributors
- Filter out low-quality ones

---

## Minimal Scoring System (for editors)
Score each from 0–5:
- Relevance
- Rigor
- Clarity
- Originality

**Publishing threshold example:**  
- Minimum score: 16/20p
